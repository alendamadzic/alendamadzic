# Things I Believe (in Product Management)

<p className="text-sm text-muted-foreground mt-0">29 June 2026</p>

- Showing beats telling. Bring a working prototype to the stakeholder meeting, not a roadmap slide.
- AI didn't remove the bottleneck, it relocated it. The hard part was never building, it's deciding what's worth building.
- Outcomes over outputs isn't a framework, it's the only test that matters once a customer is involved.
- Opinionated software still wins. AI didn't change that, it just made it harder to fake having a point of view.
- AI can de-risk feasibility almost for free now. Value risk just got harder, because customers expect more from less effort.
- AI doesn't fail to fix bad direction, it disguises it. A bad idea built in an afternoon looks as polished as a good one.
- When everyone can ship an MVP in a weekend, the MVP stops being the differentiator. Conviction is.
- PMs obsessing over whether to learn to code are solving the wrong problem. The shortage is in product sense, not in Python.
- As AI commoditises execution, PMs who built their reputation on shipping fast are about to be exposed for having nothing else.
- AI raised the bar for craft, not lowered it. Mediocre ideas ship faster now, which makes good ones easier to spot.
